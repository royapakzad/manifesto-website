Who We Are

The Iranian Women's Coalition for Internet Freedom is a collective of advocates, activists, and digital rights defenders working to ensure free and unrestricted access to the internet for all people.

We believe that internet freedom is a fundamental human right and essential for democracy, education, economic development, and social progress. Our mission is to challenge censorship, promote digital literacy, and advocate for policies that protect online freedom and privacy.

Our Mission

The coalition is led by Iranian women who speak from the positionality of a group that faces systematic legal, political, and social discrimination. Recognizing that restrictions on internet access disproportionately harm women and other marginalized communities—by limiting their ability to organize, document abuses, access information, and participate in public life—the coalition challenges the Iranian government's power to disconnect, censor, and surveil people and to cut them off from the global internet.

Our Approach

Grounded in the belief that access to the global internet is a fundamental human right, the coalition calls for an open, free, and rights-respecting internet for all, especially those most marginalized. We advocate for open and safe internet connectivity for the people of Iran—connectivity that is not controlled by the state or subject to selective interference, throttling, or shutdowns.

Woman, Life, Freedom

We consider this initiative as part of Woman, Life, Freedom—an intersectional resistance movement that imagines a life without oppression and fear for all. Our experience has taught us that when voices are silenced, imagining a different future becomes an act of resistance.
